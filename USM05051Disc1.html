 Every point in the plane is represented by an ordered pair (a, b) of real numbers a and b.
●
 The number a is called its x-coordinate and number b is called its y-coordinate. In general, a and b are called the coordinates of the point.
●
 From, O move a units the rights on x-axis.
●
 Then go b units vertically upward from a if b is positive, or b units vertically downward if b is negative.
●
 If a point lies on x-axis, then its y-coordinate (b) is 0. We write this point as A (a, 0).
●
 If a point lies on y-axis, then its x-coordinate (a) is 0 and we write this point as B (0, b).

Two intersecting number lines, one horizontal and another vertical, divide the plane into four quadrants. A coordinate system can be set up using these lines and the quadrants.
●
 The point of intersection of these lines represents the zero on both number lines.
●
 Numbers to the right of zero on the horizontal number line are positive, and to the left of zero are negative.
●
 Numbers above the zero on the vertical line are positive, and below the zero are negative.
●
 The horizontal number line is called and x-axis.
●
 The vertical line is called the y-axis.
●
 The x-axis and y-axis together and the four quadrants constitute the rectangle coordinate system.
●
 The quadrants are numbered anti-clockwise as shown is the above figure.
